Culture Shock

Mabuya Camp

Mabuya camp is one of the many tourist lodges dotted across Malawi. It was a really great stepping stone into Malwawi. The owners are from Britain and moved here a few years ago. They do great food and have 3 lovely dogs. This is where we spent a few days going through some courses

The language

So, although most educated peaople in the country speak English, lot's of rural people do not and with Malawian culture it's really important to greet people in the vernacular. Our local guide Frackson was the one to take us through this. My favourite saying so far is "Mwatandala" which means good afternoon.

Cultural awareness

Matt took us through a full 8 hour day of cultural differences. I felt lots of this was direceted to the female members of the group however I also took lots from it. Matt was very brutal with his honestly and I think that was really important. We went through lots of health and safety stuff as well. Things like Malaria etc. The biggest rule is if you see a snake, run.

Market visit

On our last day in Lilongwe we decided to go to town and the Market. This was like boxing day on steroids. All the locals were so excited to see Muzungu (white people) and wanted to sell us things. Some nice little bits, but quite a lot of random clothes etc. After a long hot walk we jumped in the lodge pool had dinner and got ready for a the big ride up to Maji Zuwa, Chilumba.
