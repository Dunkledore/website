Day Of Travel

The first steps

Early morning

So the night before I left was very long. I really didn't sleep much. I didn’t feel nervous, but I guess it was more subconscious nerves. I woke up pretty early and just generally relaxed all morning. Had a nice brunch of cranberry and Camembert with the family. Then we packed up the car and began the trip to Heathrow. Pretty uneventful- I just listened to music. At the airport we met up with Martha and Odette, our parents exchanged numbers (well, they tried) and past security we went.

The flight

After peacefully passing through security - and enjoying the look on the security guy as the bagpipes went through - we had a quick pitstop at Boots for some drinks. Then it was onto the plane to Ethiopia - the worst flight of my life. While on that plane, I did not get one bit of sleep for 8 hours. Instead, I watched War Of The Planet Of The Apes, which was terrible, and The Hitman's Bodyguard (also terrible). The food was terrible too. So yeah, that flight sucked. We then landed in Ethiopia, and from here it was pretty easy with a quick jump over to Malawi. Landing in Malawi we met up with the Canadians – an interesting bunch...especially that Amelia girl. One girl lost her bag, so a few tears there, but no biggy.

First Lattitude meet

As we left the airport we met up with Matt, Geoff and Frackson. Big smiles and lots of hellos. We all crammed into a little minibus and experienced Malawian roads for the first time - quite an experience. Thankfully we all got to our camp in one piece though.
